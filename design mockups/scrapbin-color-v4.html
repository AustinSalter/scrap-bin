07 — Trade-offs

A — Tidepool

Accent feel: Cerulean is calm, professional, recedes. Lifted signals (#60a5fa, #34d399, #fbbf24) are bright and legible at any size. The blue badge and drag-state feel Linear-crisp.

Personality: Trustworthy tool. The accent says "I'm reliable." Blue on white is the most conventional pairing — which is a strength (familiar) or weakness (generic).

B — Arcadia

Accent feel: Vermillion is warm, urgent, present. Fresco signals (#6a9ec0, #6aaa72, #c4a040) are desaturated and painterly — they coordinate rather than shout. The orange badge and drag-state feel distinctly authored.

Personality: Scholar's tool. The accent says "I have taste." Vermillion on white is rarer — which gives identity but needs careful use to avoid feeling like an error state.

Key question: Do you want the accent to recede (cerulean) or announce (vermillion)? The base is locked — white, black, Zinc. Signals are the subtler choice: lifted/bright (Tidepool) vs muted/painterly (Arcadia). The fresco signals are more distinctive but need testing at 6px dot size on white. The Arcadia cypress darks are still available as a dark mode — just swap the base tokens back.
