Key Design Decisions

Map as Home

The cluster landscape is always the center of gravity. Every screen is a mode of the landscape, not a navigation away from it. Stream is a panel you open. Search is a filter you apply. Threads are a highlighting lens. You never leave the map.

Notes are Fragments

Your commentary gets embedded and clustered the same way ingested content does. Over time, your notes become the densest points in the graph — they're the synthesis that connects everything else. Diamond markers on the map. Accent borders in lists.

No Modes, Just Panels

Stream, margin, and search are panels that slide in from the sides. The landscape adjusts. There's no Dashboard, no Cluster Explorer, no Thread Explorer — there's just the landscape with different panels visible.

What this kills from the requirements doc: Dashboard (replaced by the landscape), Terminal (never belonged), Cluster Explorer grid (the map IS the explorer), Thread Explorer list (threads are a map highlight). What survives: Settings, Search (as a map filter), Fragment Detail (margin panel expansion), Source Status (folded into Settings). The nav rail shrinks from 5 items to 3: Landscape, Stream, Settings.
